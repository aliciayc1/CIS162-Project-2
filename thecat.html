Before everything else, what is most important is making sure that the cat you adopt is ideal to what you need in a pet. Certain attributes of a cat can determine how it can interact in your home, which I will go over below.

To start, different breeds of cats can differ in behavior. For example, cats like the Chartreux and American Shorthair do not shed much hair compared to other breeds, and cats like the Ragdoll are very friendly and great for families.

In addition, the age of cats can determine how they interact as well. For instance, cats aged seven to ten are not overly active but more prone to physical problems, while cats that range from one to two years old are healthier but are more curious and high-energy.

Lastly, you should also make an observation of a cat's personality before you adopt it. Even though its breed and age will have an affect on its behavior, it may also have some traits on its own that can be helpful or harmful.
