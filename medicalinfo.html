When owning a cat, you must also keep in mind its health and make sure that it stays safe. Here are some things you should keep in mind to make sure it remains healthy as long as possible:

There are multiple important vaccinations that your cat should recieve every so often. These involve the FVRCP vaccine, the rabies vaccine, and the FeLV vaccine. A more in-depth explanation on what they are and when you should get them can be found here.

Neutering or spaying your cat is also required in some areas. This is the process of removing your cat's reproductive organs, and it can be beneficial for the population, their health, and even their behavior. However, it is recommended that your cat undergo this surgery young, from 4-6 months old.

Fleas are a major problem for cats, since they cause heavy itching and bumps, which can lead to hair loss and even diseases that can even be spread to humans. There is an easy treatment method that can be found here. To prevent fleas, your cat can get a flea vaccination or wear a collar that wards away fleas.
